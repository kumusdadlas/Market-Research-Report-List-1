<p><p>The Antisense & RNAi therapeutics market encompasses various types of RNA-based treatments targeting gene expression. RNA interference (RNAi) mechanisms reduce or silence gene activity, primarily using small interfering RNA (siRNA) to disrupt target messenger RNA. MicroRNA (miRNA) regulates gene expression post-transcriptionally, influencing various cellular processes. Antisense RNA therapies involve synthetic sequences complementary to target mRNA, preventing translation. Together, these approaches offer innovative solutions for treating genetic disorders, cancers, and viral infections, driving growth in the biotechnology sector.</p></p>
